## I. CYANOTIC CONGENITAL HEART DISEASE

### A. **Tetralogy of Fallot (ToF)** ‚úÖ **EXTENSIVELY COVERED**

#### Learning Objectives Covered:
1. **Describe the four anatomic components of ToF** ‚úÖ **Q1, Q4**
   - Pulmonary stenosis (RVOT obstruction)
   - Right ventricular hypertrophy
   - Ventricular septal defect (VSD)
   - Overriding aorta

2. **Explain squatting physiology and tet spells** ‚úÖ **Q1, Q2**
   - Q1: Squatting increases SVR ‚Üí decreases R‚ÜíL shunt ‚Üí improves oxygenation
   - Q2: Tet spells from infundibular spasm ‚Üí increased RVOT obstruction

3. **Recognize classic imaging findings** ‚úÖ **Q3**
   - Boot-shaped heart on CXR
   - Decreased pulmonary vascular markings

4. **Understand embryologic basis** ‚úÖ **Q4**
   - Anterior deviation of infundibular septum (neural crest defect)
   - Association with 22q11 deletion

5. **Know surgical management timing** ‚úÖ **Q5**
   - Early complete repair in symptomatic infants

6. **Identify characteristic murmur** ‚úÖ **Q6**
   - Harsh systolic ejection murmur at LUSB (RVOT obstruction)
   - Single S2 (diminished P2)

#### Gaps:
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Pink tet (pulmonary atresia with VSD)** - NOT COVERED
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Palliative shunts (Blalock-Taussig)** - NOT COVERED
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Long-term post-repair complications (pulmonary regurgitation)** - NOT COVERED

### B. **Transposition of the Great Arteries (d-TGA)** ‚úÖ **WELL COVERED**

## II. ACYANOTIC CONGENITAL HEART DISEASE (LEFT-TO-RIGHT SHUNTS)

### D. **Atrioventricular Septal Defect (AVSD)** ‚úÖ **COVERED**

## CONCLUSION

The existing question bank provides **excellent coverage** of many core USMLE Step 1 learning objectives for structural congenital heart disease, with particularly strong coverage of:

- Tetralogy of Fallot (comprehensive)
- Transposition of great arteries
- Genetic syndromes (Down, Turner, DiGeorge, Williams, Edwards, Patau)
- Coarctation of the aorta
- Complex cyanotic lesions (HLHS, tricuspid atresia, Ebstein, ALCAPA, obstructed TAPVR)
- Hemodynamic principles and physical examination maneuvers

However, there are **significant gaps** in coverage of:

- Aortic stenosis (critical and valvular)
- Pulmonary stenosis
- Pulmonary atresia with intact ventricular septum
- VSD natural history and complete presentation spectrum
- Additional genetic syndromes (Noonan, Marfan)
- Surgical procedures and post-operative complications
- Complete vascular ring spectrum
- Device-based and catheter-based interventions

**Recommendation:** Consider adding 10-15 additional questions to address Priority 1 gaps to achieve comprehensive USMLE Step 1 coverage for structural congenital heart disease.
