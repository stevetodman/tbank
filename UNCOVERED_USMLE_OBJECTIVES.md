## CATEGORY 1: Specific Cardiac Lesions (High Yield)

### 1.1 Pulmonary Atresia
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Very High)

**Why Important:**
- Classic ductus-dependent lesion
- Common vignette: cyanotic newborn, ductal closure
- PGE1 therapy critical

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- Differentiation between PA with intact ventricular septum vs. PA with VSD
- Embryology: failure of right ventricular outflow tract development
- Anatomy: confluence vs. non-confluence of pulmonary arteries
- Management: PGE1 → surgical palliation (BT shunt vs. RV-PA conduit)
- Association with MAPCAs (major aortopulmonary collateral arteries)

**Sample Question Topics:**
- Newborn with severe cyanosis, absent pulmonary artery on echo → diagnosis
- Distinguishing PA from critical PS on echo/catheterization
- Surgical approach selection based on anatomy

---

### 1.2 Critical Pulmonary Stenosis
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (High)

**Why Important:**
- Ductus-dependent right heart lesion
- Balloon valvuloplasty as definitive treatment

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- Clinical presentation: cyanosis that improves with PGE1
- Differentiation from pulmonary atresia
- Balloon valvuloplasty technique and indications
- Long-term complications: pulmonary regurgitation

**Sample Question Topics:**
- Infant with cyanosis, systolic ejection murmur → critical PS vs. PA
- Appropriate intervention: balloon valvuloplasty vs. surgery

---

### 1.3 Critical Aortic Stenosis
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (High)

**Why Important:**
- Ductus-dependent left heart lesion
- Causes shock in neonatal period

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- Clinical presentation: shock, poor perfusion, weak pulses
- Differentiation from HLHS
- Emergency management: PGE1 + inotropes
- Balloon aortic valvuloplasty
- Long-term sequelae: aortic regurgitation, need for Ross procedure

**Sample Question Topics:**
- Newborn with shock, absent femoral pulses → critical AS vs. HLHS
- Management approach for ductus-dependent systemic circulation

---

### 1.4 Interrupted Aortic Arch (IAA)
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (High)

**Why Important:**
- Strong association with 22q11 deletion (DiGeorge)
- Classic vignette with VSD

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- Types: Type A (distal to left subclavian), Type B (between left carotid and subclavian), Type C (between innominate and left carotid)
- Association with 22q11.2 deletion syndrome (50-80%)
- Clinical presentation: shock when ductus closes
- Upper vs. lower extremity BP differential (reverse of coarctation)
- Embryology: failure of 4th aortic arch development

**Sample Question Topics:**
- Infant with DiGeorge syndrome, VSD, and absent aortic arch segment
- BP differential: higher in right arm than legs (Type B IAA)
- Distinguishing IAA from severe coarctation

---

### 1.5 Partial Anomalous Pulmonary Venous Return (PAPVR)
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐ (Medium)

**Why Important:**
- Sinus venosus ASD association
- Scimitar syndrome variant

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- Most common: right upper lobe vein → SVC
- Association with sinus venosus ASD
- **Scimitar syndrome:** right pulmonary veins → IVC, hypoplastic right lung, dextroposition
- Scimitar sign on CXR (curved vascular shadow along right heart border)
- Often asymptomatic, found incidentally

**Sample Question Topics:**
- CXR with scimitar sign → diagnosis
- Echocardiography showing anomalous pulmonary venous drainage
- Association with sinus venosus ASD

---

### 1.6 Aortopulmonary Window
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐ (Low-Medium)

**Why Important:**
- Rare but testable differential for continuous murmur

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- Communication between ascending aorta and main pulmonary artery
- Presentation similar to large PDA or truncus arteriosus
- Differentiation from PDA: separate aortic and pulmonary valves
- Rapid progression to Eisenmenger if untreated

**Sample Question Topics:**
- Infant with large left-to-right shunt, separate semilunar valves → not truncus

---

### 1.7 Double Outlet Right Ventricle (DORV)
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐ (Medium)

**Why Important:**
- Spectrum of presentations depending on VSD location

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- Both great vessels arise from RV
- VSD location determines physiology:
  - Subaortic VSD: TOF-like physiology
  - Subpulmonary VSD (Taussig-Bing): TGA-like physiology
- Management based on VSD relationship to great vessels

**Sample Question Topics:**
- Echo showing both vessels from RV → DORV
- Clinical presentation varies by VSD location

---

### 1.8 Congenitally Corrected Transposition (cc-TGA/L-TGA)
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐ (Medium)

**Why Important:**
- Atrioventricular and ventriculoarterial discordance
- Can be asymptomatic for years

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- Ventricular inversion: morphologic RV on left, morphologic LV on right
- Atrioventricular discordance + ventriculoarterial discordance
- "Physiologically corrected" but anatomically abnormal
- Long-term complications: systemic RV failure, AV block, tricuspid regurgitation
- Association with complete heart block (age 2% per year)

**Sample Question Topics:**
- Adult with RV as systemic ventricle, progressive heart failure
- Complete heart block in young adult with CHD

## CATEGORY 2: Maternal Factors & Teratogens (High Yield)

### 2.1 Lithium → Ebstein Anomaly
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Very High)

**Note:** The repository has an Ebstein anomaly question (Part 3), but **maternal lithium exposure** may not be explicitly covered.

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- Lithium exposure in first trimester → Ebstein anomaly risk (0.05-0.1% absolute risk)
- Classic association on USMLE
- Apical displacement of tricuspid valve
- "Atrialized" right ventricle

**Sample Question Topics:**
- Pregnant woman on lithium for bipolar disorder → fetal cardiac risk
- Infant with tricuspid regurgitation, displaced leaflet → maternal medication history

---

### 2.2 Maternal Diabetes → Congenital Heart Disease
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Very High)

**Why Important:**
- Common USMLE association
- Multiple cardiac defects

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- Maternal hyperglycemia → increased CHD risk (3-5x)
- **Most common:** VSD, TGA, coarctation
- **Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy** (transient, neonatal period)
- Mechanism: hyperglycemia → oxidative stress during organogenesis
- Other associations: caudal regression syndrome, macrosomia

**Sample Question Topics:**
- Infant of diabetic mother with VSD and hypertrophic septum
- Neonatal hypoglycemia + hypertrophic cardiomyopathy → maternal diabetes
- Risk reduction: preconception glucose control

---

### 2.3 Congenital Rubella Syndrome → PDA + Peripheral PS
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Very High)

**Why Important:**
- Classic TORCH infection
- Multiple system involvement

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- First trimester rubella → PDA, peripheral pulmonary stenosis
- Classic triad: cataracts, deafness, cardiac defects
- **"Blueberry muffin" baby:** extramedullary hematopoiesis
- Prevention: MMR vaccine (contraindicated in pregnancy)
- Peripheral PS: supravalvular, branch pulmonary stenosis

**Sample Question Topics:**
- Infant with cataracts, deafness, PDA → maternal infection
- Unvaccinated mother with first trimester rash → fetal risk
- "Blueberry muffin" lesions + PDA → congenital rubella

---

### 2.4 Alcohol (Fetal Alcohol Syndrome) → VSD, ASD
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (High)

**Why Important:**
- Most common preventable cause of intellectual disability

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- Alcohol exposure → VSD, ASD (30-50% have cardiac defects)
- Facial features: smooth philtrum, thin vermilion border, short palpebral fissures
- Growth restriction (prenatal and postnatal)
- Neurodevelopmental abnormalities
- No safe level of alcohol in pregnancy

**Sample Question Topics:**
- Child with VSD, smooth philtrum, developmental delay → maternal history
- Prevention counseling for alcohol use in pregnancy

---

### 2.5 Phenytoin → TOF, VSD
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐ (Medium)

**Why Important:**
- Fetal hydantoin syndrome

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- Phenytoin exposure → TOF, VSD, PS
- Facial features: hypertelorism, broad nasal bridge, cleft lip/palate
- Distal digital hypoplasia (hypoplastic nails, small fingers)
- Epilepsy management in pregnancy: valproate has higher risk

**Sample Question Topics:**
- Pregnant woman with epilepsy → safest anticonvulsant
- Infant with TOF, cleft palate, digital hypoplasia → medication exposure

---

### 2.6 Isotretinoin (Accutane) → Conotruncal Defects
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (High)

**Why Important:**
- Highly teratogenic, requires iPLEDGE program

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- Isotretinoin → conotruncal defects (TOF, truncus, TGA)
- Thymic hypoplasia (similar to DiGeorge)
- CNS malformations (neural tube defects)
- Requires two forms of contraception + monthly pregnancy tests
- iPLEDGE program

**Sample Question Topics:**
- Woman on isotretinoin for acne → contraception counseling
- Infant with truncus arteriosus, thymic hypoplasia → maternal medication

## CATEGORY 3: Genetic Syndromes & Associations (High Yield)

### 3.1 Noonan Syndrome → Pulmonary Stenosis
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (High)

**Why Important:**
- Most common RASopathy
- "Male Turner syndrome" appearance

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- **Cardiac:** Pulmonary stenosis (50-80%), hypertrophic cardiomyopathy, ASD
- Facial features: hypertelorism, downslanting palpebral fissures, low-set ears, webbed neck
- Short stature
- Cryptorchidism (males)
- Genetics: PTPN11 mutation (most common), autosomal dominant
- Normal chromosomes (vs. Turner 45,X)

**Sample Question Topics:**
- Male child with PS, webbed neck, short stature, cryptorchidism → diagnosis
- Differentiation from Turner syndrome (chromosomal analysis)
- Association with pulmonary stenosis

---

### 3.2 Holt-Oram Syndrome → ASD/VSD + Thumb Anomalies
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐ (Medium)

**Why Important:**
- Heart-hand syndrome
- Autosomal dominant

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- Cardiac defects (ASD, VSD) + upper limb abnormalities
- **Thumb:** absent, hypoplastic, or triphalangeal
- Radial ray abnormalities
- Genetics: TBX5 mutation (transcription factor for heart and limb development)
- Autosomal dominant with variable expressivity

**Sample Question Topics:**
- Child with ASD and absent thumbs → syndrome
- Family history of cardiac defects and limb abnormalities
- TBX5 mutation

---

### 3.3 VACTERL Association → VSD
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (High)

**Why Important:**
- Mnemonic for multiple anomalies

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- **V**ertebral anomalies
- **A**nal atresia
- **C**ardiac defects (VSD most common)
- **T**racheoesophageal fistula
- **R**enal anomalies
- **L**imb abnormalities
- Association, not syndrome (sporadic, not genetic)

**Sample Question Topics:**
- Newborn with esophageal atresia, VSD, vertebral anomalies → VACTERL
- Differentiation from genetic syndromes

---

### 3.4 CHARGE Syndrome → Conotruncal Defects
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐ (Medium)

**Why Important:**
- CHD7 mutation

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- **C**oloboma
- **H**eart defects (TOF, VSD, ASD, atrioventricular canal)
- **A**tresia of choanae
- **R**etarded growth and development
- **G**enital hypoplasia
- **E**ar anomalies (hearing loss)
- CHD7 gene mutation (chromodomain helicase DNA-binding protein)

**Sample Question Topics:**
- Infant with coloboma, choanal atresia, TOF → syndrome
- CHD7 mutation

---

### 3.5 Marfan Syndrome → Aortic Root Dilation
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (High)

**Why Important:**
- Risk of aortic dissection
- FBN1 mutation

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- Aortic root dilation → aortic regurgitation, dissection
- Tall stature, long limbs (arm span > height)
- Lens dislocation (ectopia lentis) - **upward**
- Pectus excavatum/carinatum
- Mitral valve prolapse
- FBN1 mutation (fibrillin-1) → defective connective tissue
- Management: beta-blockers, losartan, serial echo monitoring
- Aortic root replacement if >5cm (or <5cm with family history of dissection)

**Sample Question Topics:**
- Tall adolescent with aortic regurgitation, upward lens dislocation → diagnosis
- Aortic root measurement, surgical criteria
- Differentiation from homocystinuria (downward lens dislocation)

---

### 3.6 Tuberous Sclerosis → Cardiac Rhabdomyoma
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (High)

**Why Important:**
- Most common fetal cardiac tumor

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- Cardiac rhabdomyoma: benign, multiple, arise from ventricles
- Often detected on prenatal ultrasound
- Association with tuberous sclerosis (TSC1, TSC2 mutations)
- Other features: facial angiofibromas, seizures, intellectual disability, renal angiomyolipomas, cortical tubers
- Natural history: rhabdomyomas often regress spontaneously
- Arrhythmias if large or obstructing

**Sample Question Topics:**
- Fetus with multiple cardiac masses on prenatal US → most likely diagnosis
- Child with seizures, facial angiofibromas, history of cardiac tumors → TSC
- Benign cardiac tumor that regresses

## CATEGORY 4: Advanced Concepts & Physiology

### 4.1 Fontan Circulation (Single Ventricle Physiology)
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐ (Medium)

**Why Important:**
- Final palliation for single ventricle lesions

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- Indications: tricuspid atresia, HLHS, other single ventricle lesions
- Staged approach:
  - Stage 1: Norwood (neonatal)
  - Stage 2: Glenn/bidirectional cavopulmonary anastomosis (4-6 months)
  - Stage 3: Fontan completion (2-4 years)
- **Fontan physiology:** passive flow from IVC/SVC → PA (no RV)
- Complications:
  - Elevated systemic venous pressure
  - Hepatic congestion → cirrhosis
  - Protein-losing enteropathy
  - Plastic bronchitis
  - Arrhythmias (atrial)
  - Thromboembolic events
- Pregnancy contraindicated (high maternal/fetal risk)

**Sample Question Topics:**
- Adult with HLHS history, elevated JVP, hepatomegaly → Fontan physiology
- Complication of Fontan: protein-losing enteropathy mechanism
- Contraindication to pregnancy

---

### 4.2 Blalock-Taussig Shunt
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐ (Medium)

**Why Important:**
- Historical and current palliation

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- Surgical connection: subclavian artery → pulmonary artery
- Indication: increase pulmonary blood flow in cyanotic lesions (TOF, PA, tricuspid atresia)
- Modified BT shunt: Gore-Tex conduit between subclavian and PA
- Continuous "machinery" murmur (similar to PDA)
- Complications:
  - Limb ischemia
  - Shunt thrombosis
  - Pulmonary overcirculation
  - Ipsilateral subclavian steal

**Sample Question Topics:**
- Infant with TOF, severe cyanosis → palliative shunt vs. complete repair
- Child with BT shunt history, weak left radial pulse → mechanism

---

### 4.3 Subclavian Steal Syndrome (with Coarctation)
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐ (Low-Medium)

**Why Important:**
- Classic vascular syndrome

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- Stenosis of left subclavian artery (proximal to vertebral origin)
- Exercise of left arm → reversal of flow in left vertebral artery
- Symptoms: left arm claudication, dizziness, syncope with arm use
- BP differential: lower in left arm
- Can occur after coarctation repair if subclavian used for patch

**Sample Question Topics:**
- Patient with syncope during left arm exercise → diagnosis
- Post-coarctation repair with arm claudication

---

### 4.4 Shone Complex (Syndrome)
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐ (Low)

**Why Important:**
- Multiple left-sided obstructions

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- Four left-sided lesions (may have some or all):
  1. Supravalvular mitral ring
  2. Parachute mitral valve
  3. Subaortic stenosis
  4. Coarctation of the aorta
- Progressive left heart obstruction
- May present at different ages depending on severity

**Sample Question Topics:**
- Child with coarctation + parachute mitral valve → syndrome
- Multiple levels of left heart obstruction

---

### 4.5 Endocardial Fibroelastosis (EFE)
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐ (Low-Medium)

**Why Important:**
- Association with left-sided obstructive lesions

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- Thickening of left ventricular endocardium (collagen, elastic fibers)
- Primary (dilated LV, heart failure) vs. secondary (obstructive lesions like AS, coarctation)
- **Association:** Critical AS, HLHS, aortic atresia
- Presentation: heart failure in infancy
- Imaging: echo shows bright endocardium
- Histology: diffuse endocardial thickening

**Sample Question Topics:**
- Infant with critical AS, bright endocardium on echo → diagnosis
- Pathology showing endocardial thickening

## CATEGORY 6: Surgical & Interventional Procedures

### 6.1 Balloon Atrial Septostomy (Rashkind)
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (High)

**Note:** May be mentioned in TGA questions but worth dedicated coverage.

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- Emergency procedure for TGA with restrictive ASD
- Balloon catheter inserted via umbilical vein → inflated in LA → pulled back to tear atrial septum
- Improves mixing between circulations

### 6.2 Arterial Switch Operation (Jatene)
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐ (Medium)

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- Definitive repair for TGA
- Performed in first 1-2 weeks of life
- Great arteries transected and switched
- Coronary arteries reimplanted (LeCompte maneuver)
- Complications: coronary ischemia, supravalvular stenosis

---

### 6.3 Norwood Procedure (HLHS)
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐ (Medium)

**Note:** Repository has HLHS question - verify if Norwood covered.

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- Stage 1 palliation for HLHS
- Creates common mixing chamber
- Damus-Kaye-Stansel anastomosis
- Modified BT shunt or RV-PA (Sano) conduit for pulmonary blood flow
- High mortality (15-20%)

---

### 6.4 Ross Procedure
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐ (Low-Medium)

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- For aortic valve disease in young patients
- Pulmonary valve (autograft) → aortic position
- Pulmonary valve replaced with homograft
- Advantage: autograft grows with patient
- Complication: eventual need for pulmonary valve replacement

## CATEGORY 7: Pathophysiology & Mechanisms

### 7.1 Eisenmenger Physiology (Detailed)
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Very High)

**Note:** Repository has Eisenmenger question - verify depth of coverage.

**Topics to Ensure Covered:**
- Chronic left-to-right shunt → pulmonary vascular remodeling
- Intimal hyperplasia, medial hypertrophy → fixed pulmonary hypertension
- Shunt reversal (right-to-left) when PA pressure > systemic
- Clinical: differential cyanosis if PDA (lower body more cyanotic)
- Contraindications: pregnancy (50% maternal mortality), surgery
- Treatment: lung transplant ± heart transplant (Eisenmenger is irreversible)
- Complications: hemoptysis, brain abscess, stroke, arrhythmias

---

### 7.2 Differential Cyanosis Patterns
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (High)

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- **Upper body pink, lower body blue:**
  - PDA with Eisenmenger (PA pressure > aortic)
  - Coarctation + PDA with R→L shunt
- **Upper body blue, lower body pink:**
  - TGA with PDA (deoxygenated blood to head/arms)
  - PPHN (covered in Part 3)
- **All blue:**
  - Most cyanotic CHD (TOF, TGA with VSD, etc.)

---

### 7.3 Hyperoxia Test (Nitrogen Washout Test)
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Very High)

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- Purpose: differentiate cardiac vs. pulmonary cyanosis
- Procedure: measure PaO2 on room air, then on 100% O2 for 10 minutes
- Interpretation:
  - **PaO2 > 250 mmHg:** pulmonary disease (V/Q mismatch improves)
  - **PaO2 < 100 mmHg:** cyanotic CHD (shunt doesn't improve with O2)
  - **PaO2 100-250 mmHg:** equivocal (may be both)
- Mechanism: intracardiac shunt prevents oxygenation of all blood

---

### 7.4 Prostaglandin Physiology in Ductus Arteriosus
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (High)

**Note:** COX inhibition covered in PDA questions - verify PGE2 physiology depth.

**Topics to Ensure Covered:**
- PGE2 synthesis pathway: arachidonic acid → COX → PGH2 → PGE2
- PGE2 receptors (EP1-4) on ductal smooth muscle
- EP4 activation → ↑ cAMP → smooth muscle relaxation
- Fetal circulation: high PGE2 (placental) + low O2 → ductus open
- Postnatal: ↓ PGE2 + ↑ O2 → ductus closure (24-48 hours)
- Premature infants: delayed closure (immature ductal tissue)

---

## CATEGORY 8: Special Populations & Complications

### 8.1 Adult Congenital Heart Disease (ACHD)
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐ (Medium)

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- Long-term complications of repaired CHD
- **Post-TOF repair:** chronic pulmonary regurgitation, RV dilation, arrhythmias (VT)
- **Post-Fontan:** hepatic fibrosis, PLE, arrhythmias
- **Post-Mustard/Senning (TGA):** systemic RV failure, arrhythmias
- **Post-coarctation repair:** recoarctation, aneurysm, HTN
- Pregnancy considerations in ACHD

---

### 8.2 Infective Endocarditis Risk in CHD
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (High)

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- **High-risk lesions:** prosthetic valves, previous IE, unrepaired cyanotic CHD, repaired CHD with residual defects
- **Low-risk (no prophylaxis needed):** ASD secundum (isolated), repaired VSD/PDA/ASD >6 months post-op
- **Current guidelines:** prophylaxis only for high-risk + dental procedures
- Most common organism: Streptococcus viridans (dental), Staph aureus (IV drug use)

---

### 8.3 Brain Abscess in Cyanotic CHD
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐ (Medium)

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- Right-to-left shunt → bacteria bypass pulmonary capillary filtration
- Polycythemia (from chronic hypoxemia) → hyperviscosity → cerebral thrombosis → abscess
- Presentation: headache, focal neuro deficits, seizures, fever
- Imaging: ring-enhancing lesion on CT/MRI
- Most common organisms: Streptococcus spp., Staphylococcus

---

### 8.4 Polycythemia in Cyanotic CHD
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (High)

**Uncovered Learning Objectives:**
- Chronic hypoxemia → ↑ EPO → ↑ RBC production
- Hct > 65% → hyperviscosity symptoms
- Symptoms: headache, dizziness, fatigue, blurred vision
- Complications: stroke, seizures
- Management: hydration, phlebotomy (if symptomatic), definitive repair

## CATEGORY 9: Differential Diagnosis Scenarios

### 9.1 Neonatal Cyanosis Differential
**USMLE Relevance:** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (Very High)

**Note:** Listed in roadmap as "planned expansion."

**Framework for Complete Coverage:**

**5 T's of Cyanotic CHD:**
1. **T**etralogy of Fallot
2. **T**ransposition of great arteries
3. **T**ricuspid atresia
4. **T**runcus arteriosus
5. **T**otal anomalous pulmonary venous return

**Plus:** Pulmonary atresia, HLHS, Ebstein, critical PS

**Diagnostic Approach:**
- History: timing of cyanosis, ductal closure
- Hyperoxia test
- ECG: axis, hypertrophy
- CXR: heart size, pulmonary markings
- Echo: definitive

**Sample Question Topics:**
- Newborn with cyanosis at birth, egg-on-string CXR → TGA
- Newborn with cyanosis + boot-shaped heart → TOF
- Newborn with cyanosis + decreased pulmonary markings → PA, critical PS

### 9.2 Heart Failure in Infancy Differential
